## CORRECTION (2026-07-04, worker-round W1 STOP investigation) — the 0/27 interpretation was a harness artifact

The worker-round W1 arms exposed a skill-name COLLISION in every deployed-context round above:
project-level fixture skills at `<project>/.claude/skills/{recall,learn}` are SHADOWED by the
user's global `~/.claude/skills/{recall,learn}` (production). The init event lists both (global
first); `Skill("recall")`/`Skill("learn")` loaded the PRODUCTION texts in every arm.

Verified two ways across all prior rounds (executor T2/T2b, R1, R2, sonnet): (1) init-event
skill lists show `learn`/`recall` twice with the global copy first; (2) candidate-only marker
strings ("REQUIRED SUB-SKILL", the write-memory pointer text) appear ZERO times in every
new-arm transcript — the only "write-memory" occurrence per transcript is the init listing of
the (uncolliding, correctly discovered) atom skill itself.

**Reinterpretation:** "0/27 arms dereferenced the atom mid-procedure" is FACTUALLY true but
causally EMPTY — no arm ever saw a text directing it to the atom. The claim "agents skip
mid-procedure reference fetches at any tier/wording" is UNSUPPORTED by these rounds; it was
never tested deployed. What remains valid: the inline smokes (58ad18a4 — atom content followed
6/6 when in context), the original-T1 instrument invalidity, the judgment-bistable fixture
finding, the sonnet CLI-error self-correction observation, and (qualified) the T3 non-fire
result — write-memory was discoverable in every arm's skill list and fired autonomously 0/6.
The "parent-skill invocation 27/27" metric stands mechanically but the invoked parents were
the PRODUCTION texts, not candidates.

**Consequence for decisions:** Joe's worker redraw was presented with the artifact-tainted
causal story; his design argument (boundaries at the wrong points; a skill-share should be a
worker at a step boundary, not a mid-procedure reference card) is independent of the artifact
and stands. The reference-card form remains UNVALIDATED deployed, not measured-dead.
Harness fix for all subsequent rounds: fixture parents get collision-free names
(`wrecall`/`wlearn`) in BOTH conditions + a fixture-copy marker line asserted in transcripts.
