## Recall at the decision moments, not only at the start

engram **`/recall glance`** (the read-only rung) surfaces vault memory you haven't loaded — most valuable
right before a wrong call locks in. It's cheap, and under-recalling — shipping past a lesson you'd already
learned — costs far more than a quick glance, so **run `/recall glance` before you proceed** at these cues:

- **Even just chatting about a proposed approach** — before you endorse or rank it — **`/recall glance` first**
  (the action is recalling, not judging what's in front of you) — a past decision may have tried and killed it.
- **Before declaring work done** — **`/recall glance` first** (the action is recalling — not a substitute
  self-check or re-inspection), then verify; the vault names the gotchas you'd otherwise ship past.
- **After a failure you can't immediately explain** — **`/recall glance` once before you start guessing** (not on
  every retry) — a past lesson may name the cause.
- **Before you start building a new approach** — **`/recall glance` prior decisions and standards first**, while
  the path is still cheap to change.

Escalate to **`/recall deep`** — the thorough rung, which also crystallizes new lessons — when the decision is
weighty or irreversible, or when `glance` flags a gap it can't resolve.

These catch *application* gaps — the lesson existed, just unrecalled at the moment.
